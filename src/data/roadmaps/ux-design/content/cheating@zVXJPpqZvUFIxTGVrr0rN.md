# Cheating

Cheating, in the context of behavior design, refers to shortcuts that produce a desired user action without relying on willpower or motivation. Instead of trying to persuade the user, the designer removes the need for a decision altogether. Defaulting, making an action incidental, and automating repetition are common cheating techniques.
