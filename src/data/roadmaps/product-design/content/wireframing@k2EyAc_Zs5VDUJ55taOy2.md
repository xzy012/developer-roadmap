# Wireframing

Wireframing is the process of creating simplified, low-fidelity layouts of a screen that show structure and placement of elements without detailed visual design. It focuses attention on functionality and flow rather than color, typography, or imagery. Wireframes are often used to get early feedback and alignment before investing time in higher-fidelity design work.
