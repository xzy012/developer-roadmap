# Technical Constraints

Technical constraints are the limitations imposed by existing systems, infrastructure, or engineering capacity that shape what a design can realistically achieve. Ignoring these constraints during design can lead to ideas that look great on paper but are impossible or very costly to build. Understanding them early allows a designer to make trade-offs deliberately rather than being surprised later in development.
