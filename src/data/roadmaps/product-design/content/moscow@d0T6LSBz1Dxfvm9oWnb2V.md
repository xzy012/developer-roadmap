# MoSCoW

MoSCoW is a prioritization method that sorts requirements into four categories: Must have, Should have, Could have, and Won't have for now. It gives stakeholders a simple, shared vocabulary for discussing what is essential versus optional within a given release. This method is often used in planning discussions where a team needs to quickly agree on scope.
