# Information Architecture

Information architecture is the practice of organizing and structuring a product's content and features so users can find what they need and understand how everything relates. It includes decisions about navigation, site maps, and how content is grouped and labeled. Good information architecture is largely invisible when done well, since users move through a product without confusion.
