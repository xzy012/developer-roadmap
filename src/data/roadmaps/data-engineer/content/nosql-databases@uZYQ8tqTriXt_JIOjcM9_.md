# NoSQL Databases
 
NoSQL databases are a category of database systems that do not use the traditional relational table model. They are designed for specific data models and access patterns, including document storage, key-value pairs, wide-column stores, and graphs. NoSQL databases are often chosen for their scalability, flexibility, and performance in high-volume or unstructured data scenarios.
